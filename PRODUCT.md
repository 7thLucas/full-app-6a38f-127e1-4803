## Product: IntakeFlow

### What it is
IntakeFlow is a patient intake tool for a small clinic. This first version is intentionally scoped to the **patient-facing landing page** — the calm, reassuring, mobile-first front door patients reach before completing their intake. It is the entry point to "the calm, no-paperwork front desk for small clinics."

### What this v1 build is (and is NOT)
- **IN SCOPE NOW:** A single patient-facing landing page only.
  - Clearly presents the clinic: name, a short welcoming intro, and a brief "what to expect" reassurance.
  - One prominent primary call-to-action button: **"Begin intake"** (button is present and visually primary; it does not yet open a working form).
  - Calm, professional, trustworthy, modern feel — warm, not clinical-cold.
- **EXPLICITLY NOT NOW (later versions — do NOT build):**
  - The intake form itself.
  - The staff queue.
  - SMS confirmations.
  - Auth, dashboards, scheduling, billing.
- **Structure for the future:** Build the landing page so the future intake form, staff queue, and SMS confirmation plug in cleanly. The "Begin intake" CTA is the natural hook where the form will later attach.

### Users
- **Primary user (now):** A patient arriving at the landing page on their own phone before a clinic visit. Patients are of all ages — clarity and legibility matter more than cleverness.
- **Buyer (context):** A small clinic owner/operator who wants a calm, low-friction front door that reassures patients.

### Brand & naming
- Product/app name shown to patients: **IntakeFlow**.
- Positioning line / tagline: **"The calm, no-paperwork front desk."**
- Supporting promise: patients complete intake from their own phone before they arrive — no clipboard, no callbacks.

### Tone of voice
- Clear, trustworthy, calm, healthcare-appropriate.
- Reassuring and low-friction for patients. Privacy-respecting language.
- Warm and human — professional but never cold or bureaucratic.

### Suggested landing-page content
- A clinic name / brand mark area ("IntakeFlow") with the tagline.
- A short welcoming intro headline, e.g. a calm "Welcome — let's get you checked in" style message.
- A concise "What to expect" reassurance: a few short points (takes a couple of minutes, do it from your phone, your details stay private, you'll get a confirmation when it's received).
- One prominent primary CTA: **"Begin intake"**.
- A quiet, trustworthy footer.

### Anti-references
- Not a cold clinical EHR or hospital portal.
- Not a busy marketing landing page with aggressive sales copy.
- No dark patterns, no clutter, no jargon.

### Strategic principles
- Patient effort is the enemy — the page must feel effortless and obvious.
- Confirmation builds trust — the page should signal reliability and privacy.
- Remove the loop, don't add a system — keep it minimal and focused on one action.
